1. Twilio console

One account, one number, two webhooks. About 15 minutes.

✓Create a Twilio account and upgrade it (pay-as-you-go)

Sign up at twilio.com/try-twilio, then add a payment method to upgrade out of trial mode (Console > Billing).

Why upgrade immediately

A trial account can only call or message numbers you have verified, prefixes every message with a trial notice, and cannot complete A2P registration. Upgrading is still pay-as-you-go: there is no plan or commitment, you only pay the per-use rates in the cost table below.

✓Buy one US local number with Voice + SMS + MMS

Console > Phone Numbers > Buy a number. Filter capabilities to Voice, SMS, and MMS, pick a local number you like, and buy it ($1.15/mo[5]).

Paste it into the generator below once you have it.

Why a local (10DLC) number and not a toll-free one

Call Mode already runs on a local number, and Text Mode shares the SAME number so you save one contact card and one monthly lease. Local numbers need A2P 10DLC registration for outbound SMS (phase 2); toll-free numbers use a different verification path and read as business robocalls when they ring you.

What answers these URLs

      
Both webhooks land on the same ceo-chat server through the Cloudflare tunnel.
      Every POST is authenticated with X-Twilio-Signature and checked against your allowlisted number before anything reaches first mate; strangers are rejected or silently dropped.

    

  






2. A2P 10DLC registration 


US carriers require this before your number may SEND texts. Inbound already works.




  
✓Know what A2P gates (and what already works)

  

    
Texting IN works the moment the messaging webhook is set: your texts and MMS photos reach first mate immediately, and the web transcript shows the replies.

    
Texting OUT (SMS replies + proactive notifications) is blocked by carriers until registration completes. Unregistered outbound US SMS has been fully blocked since Sept 1, 2023 and fails with Twilio error 30034[7] [8].

    
Why carriers require this

      
A2P 10DLC is the US carriers' registry for application-to-person traffic on local numbers.
      Registration ties your number to a verified sender (the brand) and a declared purpose (the campaign), which is what keeps legitimate low-volume senders out of the spam filters.
      Registration only gates outbound; inbound and Canada-bound traffic are unaffected[8].

    

  






  
✓Create the Customer Profile

  

    
Console > Messaging > Regulatory Compliance > Onboarding. Start the US A2P onboarding and create the profile: your name, a real email, a contact phone, and your US street address[1].

    
Reuse limits to know about

      
The email address and street address can each be used at most 10 times across all A2P brand registrations with the campaign registry, so use your own, stable ones[1].

    

  






  
✓Register the Sole Proprietor brand (~$4.50 one-time)

  

    
Choose the Sole Proprietor brand type: it is exactly for individuals without a business tax ID (EIN)[1].
    You verify with a one-time SMS code sent to a personal mobile number.

    
The OTP mobile number must NOT be a Twilio (or any CPaaS) number, can be used at most 3 times across all sole proprietor registrations, and the code must be answered within 24 hours[1]. Use your own phone.

    
Fee: about $4.50 one-time[3] [4]. Approval typically lands within minutes of the OTP[2].

  






  
✓Register the campaign ($15 one-time vetting, $2/mo)

  

    
Register one campaign with the Sole Proprietor use case. It asks for a description, sample messages, and how recipients opt in. Honest, specific answers pass vetting; here is a truthful set for this exact system:

    
Campaign description:
Personal single-user assistant notifications. My own
self-hosted AI coding assistant texts me (the account
owner, the only recipient) status updates about my own
software projects, and replies when I text it first.

    
Sample message 1:
The deploy finished and all checks are green. Want me
to merge it? Full reply: {{ORIGIN}}

Sample message 2:
PR 42 is green - ready for your review.

    
Opt-in description:
I am the sole sender and sole recipient. The service
texts only my own verified mobile number, which I
configured myself in the server allowlist. Replying
STOP halts all messages.

    
Fees: $15 one-time vetting (charged again if you resubmit after a denial) + $2.00/mo per campaign[2] [3]. Vetting is manual: plan for about 1-3 weeks[2].

  






  
✓Attach your number to the campaign

  

    
When the campaign is approved, add your purchased number to the campaign's Messaging Service. A Sole Proprietor campaign can hold exactly one 10DLC number[1] - this one.

    
Throughput you get (plenty)

      
Sole proprietor throughput is fixed at 1 message per second, with carrier caps of 1000 messages/day toward T-Mobile and 15 messages/minute toward AT&T[6].
      A personal notification stream uses a fraction of that.

5. First live tests 


One call, one text, one photo, one notification. Scripts below.




  
✓First call

  

    
Open https://ceo-chat.acb-apps.com on your iPhone and tap Call me. Your phone rings from your Twilio number.

    
Answer, enter your PIN on the keypad, wait for "You are connected", then say:

    
What is the status of the repo?

    
Expect: the spoken summary in your cloned voice, the exact reply streaming in the web transcript, and barge-in (speak over it) cutting the audio.

  






  
✓First text (works before A2P approval)

  

    
Text your Twilio number from your phone:

    
status?

    
Expect: the web transcript shows the turn labeled "you (by text)" and first mate's reply.
    Before campaign approval no SMS comes back (outbound is carrier-blocked; that is the A2P gate, not a bug) - read the reply on the web.
    After approval, the same text also gets an SMS reply: the concise summary, plus a "Full reply" link when the full response holds more detail.

  






  
✓First photo (MMS)

  

    
Text a screenshot or photo with the message:

    
what does this error mean?

    
Expect: the image lands in the repo's gitignored inbox/ dir and first mate opens and inspects it as part of its answer.

  






  
✓First proactive notification (needs A2P approval)

  

    
On the hub, with the server running:

    
cd ~/firstmate/projects/ceo-chat && npm run text-captain -- "hello captain"

    
Expect: "sent: hello captain" in the terminal and the SMS on your phone.
    This is the trigger first mate sessions use for "PR is green"-style pings; CEOCHAT_TEXT_NOTIFY=0 in secrets.env turns it off.
